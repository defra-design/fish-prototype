An automatic payment is a regular debit or credit card payment taken with your agreement to securely pay for your next fishing licence. Your bank may call this a 'recurring payment' or 'continuous payment authority'.

Payments

You can give consent to set up automatic payments for future 12 month fishing licences when buying a licence online. The Environment Agency will take payments (in pounds) until you tell us or your bank to stop them.

Payments will be taken 10 days before your 12 month licence is due to expire.

The future cost of a 12 month fishing licence may change. For example, the price may be higher because of a service-wide price increase, or lower if you become eligible for a senior licence.

Payment reminders

We will notify you by text or email one month before your next automatic payment.

If an automatic payment fails, we will send you a text or email so you can arrange to buy another licence if you want to continue to fish.

If your email address or mobile number changes, contact us to update your details.

Refunds and cancellation

To request a refund within 14 days of buying a licence, see the refund policy.

To cancel an automatic payment at any time, either contact us or use the cancellation link in your confirmation or payment reminder notification. If you cancel an automatic payment through your bank, we may not know about this until we try to take the payment.

If you want to change the number of rods or type of fish covered by your licence, you will need to cancel your automatic payment and buy a new licence.

If your eligibility for a disabled concession changes, you can cancel your automatic payment and buy a new licence.

Data security

We will store and use your data following our privacy policy.

Contact us

Contact us if you would like to change your personal details or cancel an automatic payment.
